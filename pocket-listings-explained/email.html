Pocket Listings: What They Are, How They Work, and When They Make Sense

In uncertain markets, more sellers ask about pocket listings — they don't want neighbors, friends at the club, or the broader market to see they're on the market.

A pocket listing can be the right tool for that. It can also leave money on the table. The honest answer is that you're not making a market on the home — with limited buyers seeing it, you don't get true price discovery.

This week's brief lays out the trade-offs in plain English: what an "office exclusive" actually is under NAR's Clear Cooperation Policy, how to list as one, the pros, the cons, and where the Boca Raton market stands right now across 21 luxury communities.
