A proposed place of worship on Clint Moore Road—near St. Andrew's Country Club, the Polo Club, and Woodfield—is currently under review by Palm Beach County. This article summarizes publicly available information regarding the zoning application, variance history, traffic engineering considerations, and applicable land-use regulations. This analysis is provided solely for general informational purposes regarding land use and infrastructure and is not intended to guide housing choices, influence buyer or seller decisions, or characterize neighborhood desirability.

The Development Overview

The Chabad Chai Center (Application ZV-2023-00375) proposes a 246-seat place of worship on a 2.77-acre vacant parcel on the north side of Clint Moore Road, approximately 0.3 miles east of Wagon Wheel Drive. The site sits in unincorporated Palm Beach County, where places of worship are permitted uses by right in most zoning districts under the 2009 ULDC amendments.

The property's history shapes its current development constraints. In 2002, the northern portion of the original platted lots was acquired through eminent domain to accommodate Clint Moore Road expansion and drainage requirements. This historical taking created the geometric configuration that now requires variance requests for reduced lot size, depth, and rear setback from 100 feet to 60 feet.

The Palm Beach County Zoning Commission denied the variance in November 2023, citing the "self-imposed hardship" doctrine—noting that the current owner acquired the land after the eminent domain action and that potential lot unification with adjacent parcels owned by the same entity could resolve the dimensional constraints without requiring variances.

Traffic Considerations

Traffic patterns associated with institutional uses such as places of worship vary based on facility programming, service schedules, staffing levels, and special events. Palm Beach County relies on site-specific traffic engineering studies during the development review process to assess actual impacts.

Key factors that influence traffic generation for any institutional use include peak-hour arrival and departure patterns, site design and access point configuration, operational characteristics and programming schedule, and proximity to residential areas and arterial roads. Actual impacts depend on these site-specific variables, which are evaluated during county review.

Current traffic on Clint Moore Road ranges from 16,971 to 32,385 average annual daily trips depending on segment, with peak-hour volumes of approximately 3,017 vehicles between Jog Road and Military Trail. The actual traffic contribution of any proposed facility would be subject to site-specific traffic studies and county review during the approval process.

Common traffic mitigations imposed as approval conditions include dedicated turn lanes, queuing accommodations, staggered scheduling for events, and event-specific traffic management plans. Palm Beach County routinely conditions approvals on such mitigations as part of the site plan review process.

Zoning and Legal Framework

Palm Beach County zoning permits places of worship by right in most districts, with standard requirements including one parking space per three sanctuary seats, 25-foot front setbacks, and 25-30 foot maximum height.

Applicable Federal Law

  • Religious Land Use and Institutionalized Persons Act (RLUIPA) establishes standards for equal treatment of religious assemblies with secular assemblies
  • 11th Circuit Court addressed RLUIPA standards in Gagliardi v. City of Boca Raton (2018)
  • Courts have held that zoning denials must be based on technical grounds applicable to all similar uses
  • The November 2023 variance denial was issued "without prejudice," allowing for resubmission

In Gagliardi v. City of Boca Raton, the 11th Circuit Court addressed claims related to a synagogue approval. The court's analysis focused on whether the complaints raised constituted particularized injuries under applicable standing requirements. The case illustrates the judicial standards applied to land-use decisions involving religious institutions.

Academic Research on Institutional Uses and Property Values

Academic literature has examined the relationship between institutional uses and surrounding property values. The studies referenced below address aggregate trends and do not establish causal relationships for any specific property, neighborhood, or buyer profile.

Thompson et al. (2012, Contemporary Economic Policy) examined a newly built religious facility in Omaha using pre- and post-treatment hedonic pricing models. The study concluded: "We do not find that the religious structure influenced the value of surrounding properties."

A Hamburg study of 4,832 condominium listings found properties 109-219 yards from religious buildings listed for 4.8% more than comparable homes—an effect observed across various types of religious facilities. These studies address aggregate trends and do not establish causal relationships for any specific property, neighborhood, or buyer profile.

Land-Use Process Considerations

The current variance denial means the applicant must either consolidate parcels to meet dimensional requirements or pursue alternative site configurations. This process may unfold over an extended period, with opportunities for public input through Palm Beach County's quasi-judicial zoning process.

Interested parties may monitor the application status through Palm Beach County's Planning, Zoning and Building Department public records. Traffic studies, site plans, and variance applications are subject to public review during the approval process.
