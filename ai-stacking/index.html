What Is AI Stacking

One Stack Beats One Tool

AI stacking (also called LLM stacking) is the practice of combining multiple AI tools — each chosen for what it does best — into a single repeatable workflow. No one model wins every task.

Think of your stack the way a top broker thinks of a team: a researcher, a writer, a designer, and an operations lead. AI stacking gives you all four — for less than $100/month — and runs them in parallel.

The Framework

The Four-Layer Realtor Stack

Every realtor workflow worth automating breaks down into the same four layers. Master them in order.

LAYER 01

Research

Pulls live data from the open web, public records, news, and MLS-adjacent sources. Use for property dossiers, neighborhood intel, comp narratives, and seller backgrounds.

Best in class: Perplexity Pro · ChatGPT Deep Research · Gemini Deep Research
LAYER 02

Reasoning & Writing

Synthesizes raw research into briefs, scripts, listing copy, objection handlers, and seller psychology profiles. The thinking and writing engine of the stack.

Best in class: Claude (Opus / Sonnet) · ChatGPT (GPT-5)
LAYER 03

Creative

Generates images, virtual staging, video, voiceovers, and on-brand graphics. Turns insights into marketing assets without a designer.

Best in class: Nano Banana · REimagineHome · Midjourney · Runway · ElevenLabs
LAYER 04

Automation

Connects the layers, handles voice/text follow-up, schedules, and routes leads. The glue that turns a stack into a workflow.

Best in class: n8n · Zapier · Bland AI · Make · Follow Up Boss AI

Walk into any appointment with battlefield-grade intel.

The Discipline

The Five Rules of Stacking

Follow these and your stack will compound. Break them and you'll burn $100/month for nothing.

  1. One tool per job. Don't ask Claude to do live web research. Don't ask Perplexity to write your scripts. Match the tool to the layer.
  2. Hand off cleanly. The output of one tool is the input of the next. Copy a research summary into your writing model — don't restart the research.
  3. Stack incrementally. Master Layer 1 before adding Layer 2. Most agents fail because they try to install all four layers in week one.
  4. Keep the human in the loop. AI prepares; you decide. Every output gets a 60-second sanity check before it touches a client.
  5. Document your stack. Save your prompts, your handoffs, and your recipes. A stack you can't repeat isn't a system — it's an accident.
The 30-Day Test

Pick one recipe. Run it on your next 5 transactions. Track the time you save and the conversations you win. If your stack doesn't return at least 10 hours and one extra signature in 30 days, change a layer — don't abandon the stack.
