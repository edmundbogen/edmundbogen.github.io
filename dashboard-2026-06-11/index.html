The Luxury Capital Moved South: Miami Overtakes New York in Million-Dollar Listings

For the first time in nearly a decade, New York is no longer America's capital of million-dollar listings — Miami is.

Realtor.com's 2026 luxury outlook puts the Miami–Fort Lauderdale–West Palm Beach metro at 10,513 active million-dollar listings at year-end 2025, ahead of New York's 9,216. With 26.3% of Miami's luxury demand coming from the New York metro and Florida capturing 21% of all foreign U.S. homebuyers, the winning metro runs straight through Boca Raton's country club corridor.
