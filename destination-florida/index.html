The honest urgency hook: the homestead clock

On June 2, 2026 the Florida Legislature passed HJR 1-F. If at least 60% of voters approve it on the November 3 ballot, the homestead exemption (non-school taxes) rises from $50,000 to $150,000 in 2027 and $250,000 in 2028. The catch our buyers need to hear: people who establish Florida residency after December 31, 2026 face a multi-year wait (reported four years) before they qualify for the full exemption. A clean, factual reason to plan a move now — presented as a pending ballot measure, never a guarantee, always "consult your CPA."

Sources: Florida Realtors, Fox Business, Florida Senate. Not legal or tax advice.

Aggressive marketing, conservative legal

The guardrails that keep all 13 of us safe.

Fair Housing & no steering

Seven protected classes (race, color, religion, sex, disability, familial status, national origin) — plus Florida local ordinances that may add more. We sell on objective criteria (commute, price, HOA, walkability, airport, owner-occupancy), never "this area is best for [type of person]." 55+ communities are a lawful HOPA exception — verify each one.

Referral fees & RESPA

Cross-market referral fees are broker-to-broker between licensed agents only. No paying unlicensed people for referrals. No fees to lenders/title/movers for settlement-service referrals — sponsorships must be flat-fee for real exposure, never tied to volume. 25% is customary and negotiable, not a rule.

Ad-platform rules

Meta forces housing ads into the Special Ad Category (no age/gender/ZIP, 15-mile min radius, lookalikes restricted to Special Ad Audiences). Google housing ads bar age/gender/parental/marital/ZIP targeting; city/state/radius are fine. We target by geography + intent, never by protected-class proxies.

Email, SMS & Florida ad law

Explicit SMS opt-in on the form (TCPA), STOP honored, CAN-SPAM on email. Every ad, page, and member website carries the licensed brokerage name (FREC 61J2-10.025/.026). Note: the FCC "one-to-one consent" rule was vacated Jan 2025 — but written consent for marketing texts is still required.

Before we publish: a Florida real estate attorney reviews the referral structure and disclosure language. With 13 brokerages involved, each member confirms their own broker approves participation, the ad usage, and the referral terms.
