Compliance Quick Reference

AI phone agents are powerful -- but they come with legal responsibilities. This is not optional. Review this checklist before going live and revisit it quarterly.

Before You Go Live -- Mandatory Checklist

Recording Disclosure: Program your AI to disclose recording at the start of every call. Example: "This call may be recorded for quality assurance."
AI Identification: Program your AI to identify itself as an AI assistant. This is recommended now and may soon be legally required in many states.
Outbound Consent (FCC 2025 Rule): Only use AI for outbound calls to contacts with documented 1-to-1 consent. Mass robocalling with AI voices is illegal.
Fair Housing Compliance: Review all AI scripts to ensure no questions touch on protected classes (race, color, religion, sex, national origin, familial status, disability).
Call Logs: Maintain complete call logs and recordings for compliance documentation.
Opt-Out Instructions: Add clear opt-out instructions to all AI-initiated outbound communications.

Two-Party Consent States

In these states, all parties on the call must consent to recording. Your AI must disclose recording at the start of every call. If you operate in or receive calls from any of these states, this is mandatory.

California • Connecticut • Delaware • Florida • Illinois • Maryland • Massachusetts • Michigan • Montana • Nevada • New Hampshire • Oregon • Pennsylvania • Washington

Best Practice: Default Recording Disclosure

Even if your state only requires one-party consent, always disclose recording at the start of every call. It builds trust, protects you legally, and future-proofs your operation as regulations evolve.

Note on AI Regulation

AI phone regulations are evolving rapidly. The FCC's 2025 ruling on AI-generated voices in robocalls is just the beginning. Stay current by reviewing FCC and state-level guidelines quarterly. When in doubt, consult your broker's legal team.
