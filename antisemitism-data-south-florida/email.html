Antisemitism, Migration, and South Florida: What the Data Shows — and What It Doesn't

Most weeks this space is about inventory and pricing. This week I take on a harder subject — with data, with sources, and with honesty about what the numbers can and cannot tell us.

The rise in antisemitism since 2019 is one of the most thoroughly documented social trends in America — the ADL recorded 9,354 incidents in 2024, its highest total ever, and Florida's totals remain more than triple their 2019 baseline. National surveys show a community that feels less safe and is changing its behavior.

But is it measurably driving migration to South Florida, or demand in our housing market? The honest answer is more nuanced than the headlines — and this week I break down exactly what the public evidence supports, and what it doesn't.
