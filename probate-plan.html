Section 2

How Probate Works — What You Need to Know

Understanding the process is what separates you from every other agent. Here's what matters for you as the listing agent.

South Carolina (Your Primary Market)

Estates with real property or assets exceeding $45,000 must go through full probate. The personal representative (PR) — also called the executor — is court-appointed and is the only person who can sign listing agreements and sale documents on behalf of the estate. Key milestones: the will must be filed within 30 days of death, the PR must file an inventory within 90 days, and creditors have 8 months to file claims. Most PRs won't be ready to list until 6-8 months after the death — your marketing needs to nurture leads during that entire window.

Critical detail: under SC law (§ 62-3-711), the PR cannot sell real property unless the will specifically authorizes the sale, the sale is court-authorized under supervised administration, or court-ordered sale procedures are followed. Always confirm power of sale before taking the listing.

Georgia (Cross-Border Opportunity)

Georgia moves faster — the creditor claim period is only 3 months versus SC's 8. However, Georgia has a unique "Year's Support" provision that allows a surviving spouse to petition the court to set apart estate property for their support, taking priority over nearly all other claims. Always check whether a Year's Support petition has been filed before listing any Georgia probate property. Title companies generally won't insure title from Common Form probate until 4 years have passed — Solemn Form is strongly preferred.

What You Need Before Listing Any Probate Property

Certified Letters Testamentary (or Letters of Administration) from the probate court
Certified death certificate
Copy of the will — confirm whether it grants power of sale
GA only: Confirmation that no Year's Support petition is pending
Seller listed as "The Estate of [Decedent's Full Name]" — signed by the PR in fiduciary capacity

Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your Group

1

Choose a Name That Sticks

The name should feel like a community hub, not a real estate page. It needs to be something people would actually search for and want to join — not "Delaney Morgan's Listings." Here are some strong options:

RECOMMENDED

New to Columbia, SC & the Midlands

Mirrors the proven "New to Boca Raton" model. Attracts relocators, newcomers, and people exploring the area — all potential buyers and sellers.

ALTERNATIVE

Living in Columbia, SC — Insider Tips & Community

Broader net. Captures existing residents who may be selling, downsizing, or dealing with family property transitions.

ALTERNATIVE

Columbia SC Homeowners & Neighbors

Positions the group around homeownership — naturally attracts people who own, rent, or are considering buying. Estate/probate questions surface organically.

2

Set It to Approval-Only

This is critical. Set the group to require membership approval with screening questions. This keeps quality high by filtering spam, and — more importantly — gives you a built-in lead qualification system. Every person who requests to join hands you data before you've ever spoken to them. Start public until you hit ~500 members for maximum discovery in search, then switch to private to improve engagement quality.

3

Set Your Screening Questions (This Is the Gold)

Facebook lets you ask up to 3 questions when people request to join. These are your free lead qualification system:

Q1

"Welcome! What neighborhood or area of Columbia do you live in (or are you moving to)?"

Tells you their location and whether they're relocating — a huge buying signal.

Q2

"Do you currently rent, own your home, or are you planning to buy?"

This is the money question. "Planning to buy" or "own" goes straight into Follow Up Boss for follow-up. Renters may convert later — they go on your future newsletter list.

Q3

"What's your email? (Optional — we send a welcome guide with local tips!)"

Not everyone will answer, but those who do give you permission to add them to your email list. Over time this builds your newsletter audience for free.

4

Set Group Rules and Pin Them

Keep it simple: no spam, no self-promotion without admin approval, be respectful, keep it relevant to the Columbia/Midlands area. Pin the rules as the first post. This keeps the group clean and positions you as the trusted moderator.

5

Seed It With Your Existing Network

You have 1,200 contacts in Follow Up Boss. Invite your sphere, past clients, and local connections. Have your VAs help cross-promote in existing Columbia-area Facebook groups. Share the group link on your personal page and Instagram. Growth target: 100 members in 30 days, 500 in 90 days.

6

Commit to 15–30 Minutes a Day

This is a real commitment — but it's not forever at this intensity. Once the group gains momentum and members start posting on their own, your daily time drops. For now: 15-30 minutes a day to post content, approve members, review screening answers, and respond to comments. Your VA can help with approval processing and logging the screening data into your CRM.

7

Export and Process Screening Answers Weekly

Every week, review the screening answers from new member requests. Anyone who says "planning to buy" or "own" gets added to Follow Up Boss with a "Facebook Group" tag. Anyone who provides an email gets added to your future newsletter list. This is your lead pipeline — it fills itself as the group grows.

The Key Insight: This Is NOT a Real Estate Page

No "just listed" posts. No "just sold" posts. No open house announcements. Those belong on your business page. This group is about the community — and because you're the trusted person running it, when someone in the group (or someone they know) needs to sell an inherited property, deal with a family estate, or buy their next home, you're the first person they think of.

Probate leads will surface organically. Someone will post "my mom passed away and I'm not sure what to do with her house." Someone will ask about estate sales. Someone will mention they're helping a family member settle an estate. You'll already be in the room because you built the room.

Section 4

Your Content Library — 10 Topics People Actually Want to Read

The group lives or dies on whether people find it useful. The 80/20 rule applies — 80% pure community value, 20% or less anything related to real estate. Here are 10 recurring themes to rotate weekly. Build a library of these and you'll never run out of things to post.

TOPIC 01

Local Restaurant & Business Spotlights

"Have you tried [restaurant]? Here's what makes it a Columbia hidden gem." Feature a different local business each week. Owners will share your post, bringing their followers into your group.

TOPIC 02

Weekend Events & Things to Do

Post a weekly roundup — farmers markets, festivals, concerts, family events. Become the community calendar. People will check the group every week just for this.

TOPIC 03

Neighborhood Deep-Dives

"What's it really like to live in [Shandon / Forest Acres / Irmo / Lexington]?" Schools, walkability, vibes, what locals love. High-value for newcomers, positions you as the area expert — no listing pitch required.

TOPIC 04

Polls & "This or That" Questions

"Best pizza in Columbia — go!" or "Lake Murray or downtown living?" Polls drive massive engagement. Every comment boosts your post in the algorithm and grows the group.

TOPIC 05

Homeowner Tips & Seasonal Maintenance

"5 things every SC homeowner should do before hurricane season." Practical, non-salesy, genuinely useful. Homeowners save these posts — that signals to Facebook your group is valuable.

TOPIC 06

Local Market Updates (Plain English)

Once a month, share a simple market update — conversational, not a data dump. "Homes are moving faster in [area] this month — here's what that means if you're thinking about a move." No charts. No jargon.

TOPIC 07

"Ask Me Anything" Threads

Monthly open Q&A: "I'm a local broker — ask me anything about buying, selling, property taxes, or navigating real estate in Columbia. No judgment, no pitch." The probate questions will come.

TOPIC 08

Vendor & Contractor Recommendations

"Need a good plumber / electrician / estate sale company?" Post it as a question: "Who's your go-to handyman?" These threads get massive engagement and bookmarks — and they build your vendor network for probate work.

TOPIC 09

Family & Parenting in Columbia

You're a new mom — this is your superpower. "Best parks for toddlers," "daycare recommendations," "family-friendly restaurants." This taps into your mommy network sphere and attracts young families who are buyers.

TOPIC 10

Estate & Life Transition Resources

"If you're helping a family member settle an estate, here's a simple checklist." or "5 things most people don't know about inheriting a home in SC." Post these 1-2 times a month. Educational, empathetic, and they position you as the probate expert — woven naturally into a community group, not shouted from a billboard.

The Content Ratio

Out of every 10 posts, 8-9 should be pure community value (Topics 1-9 above). Only 1-2 should touch real estate — and even those should be educational, not promotional. If the group feels like a sales funnel, people leave. If it feels like the best community page in Columbia, people invite their friends. That's how you grow to 5,000, 10,000, 15,000 members — the same way I did.

The Rest of Your Playbook

The Facebook group is your engine. These are the supporting pieces that make the probate niche work.

Build Your Referral Network

Attorneys are the highest-value referral source. When a family asks their attorney "do you know a good realtor?", you want to be the only name.

Meet 5–10 probate attorneys in the first 90 days. Your pitch: "I handle the real estate side so you and your clients don't have to worry about it — I understand probate timelines, court requirements, and can provide free CMAs for estate properties."
Key SC contacts: Richland County Probate Court (Judge Amy McCulloch), Collins Family & Elder Law, local estate planning firms.

Connect with estate sale companies. After the estate sale, the house needs to be sold — you become their go-to referral. Attend local estate sales to meet operators and families.
Join the Augusta Estate Planning Council (augustaepc.org) — attorneys, CPAs, trust officers, and financial planners in one organization.

Create Educational Content (Free)

Build a probate page on your website — optimized for "selling inherited property Columbia SC" and related searches.
Create 8–12 YouTube videos in 90 days. Record on your phone. "How to Sell an Inherited House in Columbia, SC," "5 Mistakes Executors Make," "Probate Timeline in SC." Consistency beats production quality.
Record a video interview with a local probate attorney. Provides content AND often leads to attorney referrals.
Create an "Executor's Guide" PDF — share in your group, email to leads, offer to attorneys to hand to their clients.

Scripts That Open Doors

For Personal Representatives

"Hi [Name], my name is Delaney Morgan. I understand you may be dealing with some decisions about the property at [address] as part of settling an estate. I work with families in situations like yours, and I wanted to see if I could help make that process easier. I'm not looking to pressure you — I'm just a resource. Would it be okay if I shared a free guide we put together for executors in South Carolina?"

For Attorneys

"I specialize in probate real estate in the Columbia area and I'd love to be a resource for your clients who need help with property. I handle cleanouts, coordinate with estate sale companies, manage repairs, and ensure the sale process goes smoothly so your clients can focus on the legal side. Could I drop off some materials and take you to coffee?"

Use language like "I help families navigate property transitions." Acknowledge grief before discussing business. Be patient — probate takes 6-12+ months, and rushing families destroys trust.
