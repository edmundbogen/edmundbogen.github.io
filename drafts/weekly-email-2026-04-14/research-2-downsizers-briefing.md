# Research Piece 2: The Downsizer's Dilemma – Research Briefing

Received: 2026-04-14

## Key New Data Points (beyond Research 1)

### Psychology
- 2018 real-estate interviews: top barrier was emotional attachment; sellers spoke of "leaving their beloved home behind"
- AHURI grouped barriers: dwelling/location, financial, psychological (decluttering stress, losing neighbors)
- Decision Lab: endowment effect causes sellers to set prices higher than market valuations
- NAR 2025: buyers over 60 cite proximity to family as #1 reason for buying, smaller home #2

### Mortgage Lock-In (More Precise)
- FHFA Jan 2026: 20.0% below 3%, 31.5% between 3-4%, 17.1% between 4-5%, only 21.2% above 6%
- $1.9M home at 80% LTV: $6,408/mo at 3% vs $9,607/mo at 6.5% = $3,199/mo difference ($38K/yr)
- $2.2M home: $7,420 at 3% vs $11,124 at 6.5% = $3,704/mo difference
- $1.6M home: ~$2,694/mo difference
- AmeriSave: 2-1 and 3-2-1 buydown structures, sellers/builders fund escrow

### Insurance (More Specific)
- Insurance.com March 2026: avg Florida premium $7,562/year (HIGHEST IN NATION)
- Since 2020: 12+ insurers left state or went insolvent
- 2025 HB 5013 reduced reinsurance programme funding
- Citizens: 5.6% rate decrease for 2025
- Surplus lines: policy counts up 22% but premiums grew only 5% = avg 14% decline
- $300K-$400K coverage: premiums fell from $2,959 to $2,724
- Luxury ($1-5M coverage): premiums still $3,700+ range

### Hidden Costs (Specific Numbers)
- NAHB: 10.8% of home operating costs = maintenance
- Budget 1% of value/year; older homes 3-5%
- Roof replacement: $5.8K-$13K; HVAC: $10K-$14K; foundation: $4K-$30K
- Palm Beach County effective tax rate: 1.9%; Boca Raton: 1.67%
- $2.2M home = ~$36,740/yr taxes vs $1.3M villa = ~$21,710/yr ($15K difference)
- St. Andrews: $450K non-refundable membership fee + $50K POA contribution + $50,566 annual dues (plus tax) + $15K+ POA assessments
- Housekeepers for wealthy families: $75K-$128K/yr ($3,200-$5,500/mo)

### Equity Opportunity (Refined)
- AAA 10-yr munis: 3.00%, 20-yr: 4.00%, 30-yr: 4.35%
- AA 20-yr munis: 4.25%
- Treasuries Mar 2026: 3-mo 3.67%, 2-yr 3.79%, 10-yr 4.32%
- Top CDs Apr 2026: 4.25% (5-mo), 4.20% (9-mo), 4.15% (11-mo)
- Scenario: $2.2M sell, $1.3M buy, 8% selling costs + 5% purchase costs = ~$659K net equity
  - At 4.32%: ~$28,469/yr
  - At 4.25%: ~$28,008/yr
  - At 4.15%: ~$27,349/yr

### Market Data (ILHM March 2026)
- Boca/Delray single-family luxury (>=$1.2M): BALANCED, 17% sales ratio
  - 617 active listings, 104 closed (up 35% MoM)
  - Median sale price: $2,137,500 (up 15%)
  - Median 49 DOM, 94.71% sale-to-list ratio
  - $675/sqft
- Attached luxury (>=$500K): BUYER'S MARKET, 11% sales ratio
  - 569 active, 65 sold
  - Median $1,225,000 (up 45%), 65 DOM, 95.68% sale-to-list
- Jan 2026: 606 active, 111 closed, 18% ratio, busiest band $1.5-$1.7M (45% ratio)
- St. Andrews: estates $2M-$6M (3,500-7,000 sqft), villas $1M-$1.5M (1,800-2,800 sqft)

### National Trends
- Boomers in 1-2 adult households own 28% of US homes with 3+ bedrooms
- Millennial families with children own only 16% of large homes
- 57.8% of Boomer homeowners have NO mortgage
- US household mobility rate: 11.2% in 2024 (lowest in 2-decade history)
- JCHS: rates would need to drop near zero to restore 2020 affordability

### Expert Voices
- Riordan Frost (JCHS): "new normal of a less mobile nation"
- Daniel McCue (JCHS): rates would need to fall to nearly zero for median affordability
- Dana Anderson (Redfin): Boomers holding large homes because paid off or ultra-low rates
- Cameron Findlay (AmeriSave): buydowns appeal to buyers expecting rate declines in 3-4 years
- AHURI: older homeowners fear social isolation after leaving familiar neighborhoods
