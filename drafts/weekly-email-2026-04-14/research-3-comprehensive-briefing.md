## Bottom Line
Affluent homeowners in Boca Raton's country club communities face a confluence of psychological, financial, and market forces that make downsizing feel irrational — even when the math says otherwise. Rate lock-in, insurance chaos, identity attachment, and hidden carrying costs exceeding $130,000–$190,000 annually on a $2.2M luxury home create a paralysis that the FHFA estimates has prevented 1.72 million home sales nationwide since mid-2022. Yet for a homeowner who acts, the combined annual financial benefit of downsizing within the same community can reach $62,000–$77,000 per year, and upward of $100,000–$128,000 when leaving a mandatory-membership club.
