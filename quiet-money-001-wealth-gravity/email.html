Wealth Gravity

Why the world's wealth is sorting itself — and where St. Andrews fits in the map being redrawn.

The global UHNW population reached 713,626 in 2026, up from 551,435 in 2021 — roughly 89 new $30M-plus households created every day for five consecutive years. The United States generated 41% of that growth. Not 41% of the new dollars. 41% of the new individuals.

Inside the U.S., that capital is sorting itself geographically with unusual speed. Florida and Texas are absorbing the bulk of the interstate migration. Within Florida, Palm Beach County has quietly been outperforming Miami-Dade on a per-capita basis. Within Palm Beach County, Boca Raton occupies a specific position. Within Boca Raton, St. Andrews occupies its own.

This issue launches a recurring column: 2,900 words on the forces pulling capital toward South Florida, the wealth tiers redefined for 2026, the Knight Frank data most readers haven't seen, and what it all means for the homes inside this community over the next decade. Eight interactive charts. No call to action. The credibility is the marketing.
