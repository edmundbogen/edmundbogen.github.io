Condition & Market Positioning

How Today's St Andrews Buyer Will See This Home

The St Andrews Country Club luxury buyer in 2026 is overwhelmingly shopping for new construction or fully renovated homes in the $4M–$12M+ range. A 1985-vintage home with original kitchen, baths, mechanicals, and marble flooring will be evaluated by this buyer in one of two ways:

  • Renovation play: Full interior gut to the studs, likely $800K–$1.5M in renovation cost to bring to today's luxury standard
  • Teardown play: Demolish the existing structure (~$75K–$125K demo) and build new on the waterfront lot — St Andrews new construction runs ~$500–$700/sq ft

In either scenario, the buyer's willingness-to-pay is anchored to the value of the land, with only modest value attributed to the existing 40-year-old improvements. Pricing must reflect this reality to attract a qualified buyer and avoid a prolonged listing cycle.

Waterfront Premium Assumption

For this CMA, I am applying a conservative 10% waterfront premium to lot value rather than the 15–25% premium the market has historically paid. Three reasons:

  • The waterfront at this address is a community lake / golf-course water feature, not deep-water ocean or Intracoastal — buyers pay less for this view type
  • Insurance cost and elevation pressures have compressed the waterfront premium at South Florida interior communities
  • Pricing conservatively protects the seller from chasing the market down — St Andrews months of inventory has risen vs. 2022

This price positions the home at the intersection of renovation and teardown buyers — attracting the widest pool and creating competitive pressure. It reflects the home's honest as-is condition while still protecting full waterfront lot value plus a meaningful improvement contribution.

At $2,295,000, the home is positioned:

• Approximately $408/sq ft of living area — well below current St Andrews renovated pricing ($700–$1,100/sf) — so the numbers make sense to a renovation buyer
• Above land-only teardown floor (~$1.88M) — preserving value for the existing structure, pool, and improvements
• Below the 2025 tax market value of $2.67M — honest with buyers who will check PAPA and avoids the classic "overpriced" signal
• Targeted close range: $2.05M–$2.20M — with 5–10% negotiation room built in

Andrea — based on the analysis above, my recommendation is to list at $2,295,000, with the expectation of closing somewhere in the $2.05M–$2.20M range. This captures full waterfront lot value, a reasonable contribution for the existing structure, and leaves appropriate room to negotiate with a qualified buyer.


The St Andrews market in 2026 rewards sellers who price honestly. Homes that come out at aspirational numbers — chasing 2021 peak comps — are sitting, taking price reductions, and ultimately closing below where they would have if priced correctly from day one. Pricing at $2,295,000 creates genuine buyer interest from both the renovation pool and the builder/developer pool, and gives us the best chance of a clean, efficient transaction.


If you would like to consider targeted pre-listing improvements — fresh paint, kitchen/bath refreshes, refinished floors, or lighting updates — we can revisit the recommended price upward. In most cases, however, at this condition and vintage the return on pre-listing renovation is poor; the buyer will want to do it their way regardless.
