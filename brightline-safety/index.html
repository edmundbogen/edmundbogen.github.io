As South Florida residents, we live alongside one of America's newest high-speed rail systems. While Brightline represents significant progress in regional transportation, its safety record demands our attention and awareness.

A joint investigation by WLRN and the Miami Herald recorded 196 people killed by Brightline trains between the railroad's 2017 launch and December 2025. That averages to one death approximately every 13 days. The victims have ranged from teenagers to senior citizens, and the incidents have touched communities throughout Palm Beach, Broward, and Miami-Dade counties.

Importantly, Brightline has not been found at fault for any crash to date. Federal Railroad Administration investigations concluded that none of the fatal incidents resulted from train or equipment malfunction. The primary causes are trespassing, reckless driver behavior at crossings, and suicides.

"Many collisions occurred at at-grade crossings with only two gates, allowing motorists to drive around barriers."

— WLRN Investigation

New Safety Regulations

Florida's new Railroad Crossing Safety Manual took effect January 1, 2026, converting previously optional measures into mandatory requirements for new rail projects. The regulations now mandate four-gate "quad-gate" systems, raised concrete medians, LED lighting, warning horns, and improved signage at crossings.

However, these rules do not apply retroactively to existing Brightline crossings. As of late 2025, roughly 31% of crossings still lacked quad-gate systems. Upgrading existing infrastructure will take years and significant investment.

What's Being Done

The federal government awarded Florida $45 million in 2022, combined with $35 million in local matching funds, for fencing, signs, and safety features. Florida lawmakers have also allocated $100 million in state funds for rail safety improvements.

Fencing has proven effective elsewhere. After SunRail installed fences along its Central Florida corridor, illegal crossings dropped by 91%. Some municipalities, including Pompano Beach, have begun installing fencing using federal grants.

Legal Considerations

Under Florida Statutes §351.03, railroad companies must exercise reasonable care at highway crossings and maintain warning signs. Families of victims have filed wrongful-death suits alleging negligence in signage maintenance and visibility. Florida follows a modified comparative fault system—plaintiffs found more than 50% at fault cannot recover damages.

Staying Safe

The most important message: never attempt to beat a train. Brightline trains travel at speeds up to 79 mph in urban areas and can take more than a mile to stop. Never drive around lowered gates, and never walk on or near the tracks.

If you or someone you know is struggling with suicidal thoughts, please call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988. Help is available 24/7.

As our community continues to grow and transportation infrastructure expands, staying informed about safety considerations remains essential. I share this information not to alarm, but to ensure our neighbors have the awareness they need to stay safe.
